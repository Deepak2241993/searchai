3) Consider hiring a nanny with a basic educational background.

India is a developing country. You may come across many profiles of reliable nannies who are not educated enough.

About 80-90% of the nannies working in India are either uneducated or merely 8/10th pass. And there are varied reasons for this.

However, despite such a situation, it would be nice to find someone with at least some basic reading and writing knowledge or an understanding of the national/regional language.

There are several tasks in the house which require reading and writing.

Also, she should be able to call the relevant people and inform the authorities in emergencies.

4) Prefer a nanny who is clean and hygienic.

The other thing which is crucial for your child is the nanny’s health.

And if your child is going to spend around 9-10 hours of his/her day with the nanny, ideally, she should have very high standards of hygiene and sanitation.

So, before hiring a reliable nanny, ensure she knows how to maintain hygiene.

Her hair should be properly tied, and her nails should be neatly cut.

Also, try to gauge if she has a habit of washing her hands before starting work.

Ensuring the hygiene of everyone around a child is a basic necessity that keeps your child protected from any possible illness or viruses.

5) Gather a little information on the nanny’s family background. It helps.

Workers come from different backgrounds and face different challenges in life, many of which we can’t even think of.

In such a situation, if you know, just a little more about the person you are hiring as a nanny for your child goes a long way.

First, it helps you know the nanny better.

Second, it helps you bridge the gaps between the lifestyles because when a nanny stays at your place the whole day, one must accommodate and understand your home’s nuances.

Lastly, it helps the nanny establish a comfortable relationship with you as her employer because then she can freely discuss the issues & challenges she may go through in her life while attending to your child.

Although, it can get a little difficult to find out her family’s background.

But you can at least try to make some normal inquiries about the number of family members – the husband, father, his work, mother, siblings, the native village/town, her children (if any), etc.

This would help you have a better understanding of her background.

6) Clarify the monthly salary, work schedules, leaves, and days off (and exceptions as well).

Before hiring, take enough time to define your needs and expectations clearly.

Consider your child’s age, specific care requirements, and any household duties you’d like the nanny to handle.

Accordingly, define the responsibilities, work schedules, leaves and days off.

Likewise, define the monthly salary clearly. After all, money is the main reason a person will show interest in working as a nanny at your place.

A clear understanding of your expectations will help you make the other person understand it effectively.

Sometimes, parents do not bother to clarify the tasks to the nanny. Such ambiguity creates confusion later.

Of course, after a couple of weeks, when the Nanny settles in at your place, you can adjust as per each other’s life.

Furthermore, always allow your Nanny a little leeway on things – for example, 15-30 minutes on time because, just like you, she must complete her work at her home before she rushes to your home.

Your consideration and understanding will ensure that the Nanny performs her duty to the best of her abilities.

Also, the Nanny will need leaves just like you do.

There can always be some family emergency which the Nanny might have to attend to.

And at such times, you would be left with no choice but to take a leave yourself.

Be prepared for such unexpected circumstances beforehand, and don’t panic.

If possible, discuss the issue with the Nanny before hiring her and agree on the maximum number of days you can give her unplanned leaves in a month.

7) Make schedules and ask her to follow it diligently.

Define a detailed job description that outlines her responsibilities.

Be specific about your child’s routines, dietary needs, and other important information.

Such clarity will always serve as a guide for both you and the nanny, ensuring everyone is on the same page.

Emphasize the importance of discipline before you hire the Nanny.

8) Consider a trial period.

Consider a trial period before making a long-term commitment to the new nanny.

A brief trial period allows you and the nanny to assess whether the arrangement fits you and her well.

During this period, pay attention to how well the nanny interacts with your child and handles daily responsibilities.

9) Help build familiarity and adjustment with your child.

Even if you have decided to hire a particular Nanny, your child might not like her instantly.

Your kid will always need time to adjust to the new nanny in their lives.

So ask the new nanny to start working a couple of weeks along with you. You help her bridge the gap.

This way, you can supervise her work and ensure your kid gets adjusted to the new nanny in your way and you want it to be.

10) Trust your new nanny with things.

Trust is hard to come instantly but is the most important aspect of any kind of work arrangement.

When you hire a Nanny for your kids, you entrust a huge responsibility to her.

Hence, the nanny who does this should be strong, trustworthy, reliable and capable enough.

During your selection process, whatever questions you ask and the discussions you do, you will have to make your judgement.

And when you’ve hired her, trust her with every responsibility you have given her.

And while you trust, keep a check as well.

11) Trust your instincts, always.

Trust your gut feelings.

If something doesn’t feel right or you have reservations, take them seriously with your new nanny.

It is important to feel confident in your choice of nanny, as this person will play a significant role in your child’s life.

Everything mentioned above is on one side, and your instincts are on the other.

Every woman’s instincts are always very strong. Even if you miss any of the abovementioned aspects, follow your instincts.

Your instincts would lead you to the right judgment and right direction automatically.
